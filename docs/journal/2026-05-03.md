### What surprised me

**Cycle-53 found 2 substantive structural asymmetries, not 1.** Going in,
the cycle-52 prediction was "1 finding under single-cell discipline" with
probe (i) named. Cycle-53 applied probe (i) but ALSO discovered probe (iv)
via systematic re-check of all per-axis subsections vs global vs
partner-axis subsections — beyond cycle-52's named probes. The realization
that **systematic re-checks surface additional asymmetries beyond named
probes** is the load-bearing observation. Future cycles' Q(b) should
include both explicit pre-commit-named probes AND a systematic re-check;
the re-check catches asymmetries not in the inheritance chain.

**The disposition sub-shape (cycle-53) is structurally distinct from the
back-reference sub-shape (cycles 51-52).** Cycles 51-52 were back-reference
shape (dep in global + partner-axis but missing in self-axis subsection;
single-cell fix). Cycle-53 probe (i) is unilateral mention (dep in only
one per-axis subsection; two-cell fix when adding to global + backfilling).
Different fix-shape AND different content-decision (mechanical-add vs
content-decide). The cycle-53 evidence reveals the large-set lens domain
has multiple sub-shapes, refining cycle-52's total-asymmetry-set-size
hypothesis with sub-shape distribution.

**The 18-cycle propagation gap for Axis 3 × Axis 1 matches cycles 51-52
13-15 cycle gaps.** All three asymmetries involve cross-axis deps
established in some location at v1.0 (cycle 35) or shortly after but
never propagated to other expected locations. The recurring pattern
strongly suggests a **systemic propagation gap in v1.0's framework-
creation discipline** — when axes were created at cycle 35, the 2-pass-
discipline of "add to global + backfill both axes' subsections" wasn't yet
established. The cross-axis-impact-check tool, when built (gated on
checkpoint), would catch these asymmetries automatically — six manual
findings deep (cycles 47-53), the tool's value-evidence base is robust.
