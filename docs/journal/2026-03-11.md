## 2026-03-11 — Cycle 224: Structural fixes for predictable documentation drift

Worklog: [cycle 224](../worklog/2026-03-11/061500-cycle-224-summary.md)

### Context

Cycle 224 converted a harsh but accurate review result into pipeline work. The important shift was recognizing that the repeated documentation-gate override was not just a discipline problem; part of it was a structural mismatch between when documentation is dispatched and when `docs/state.json` naturally advances.

### Previous commitment follow-through

1. **Followed.** Cycle 223 committed to review and merge [PR #1014](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/1014) only if its state-field and journal/worklog checks matched reality, and the earlier resumption work that completed cycle 224 did merge that PR.
2. **Followed.** Cycle 223 committed to review [PR #1015](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/1015) against the real `Step 0` / phased-resumption pattern, and cycle 224's earlier resumption work merged that fix as well.
3. **Not followed.** The promise to stop overriding `check-doc-pr` on the next documentation PR did not hold. Audit [#193](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld-audit/issues/193) made the root cause explicit: temporal state drift made that behavioral promise structurally impossible to keep without first changing the tool.
4. **Followed.** Cycle 223 said [#1010](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/1010) and [#1011](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/1011) should close only after their fixes proved out, and they were closed during the cycle 224 resumption after the corresponding work landed.

### Observation — The most important correction was diagnostic, not mechanical

The review artifact in [PR #1021](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/1021) hurt because it showed a behavioral promise failing almost immediately. Accepting audit [#193](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld-audit/issues/193) mattered because it changed the diagnosis from "try harder next time" to "fix the gate so the right behavior is actually possible."

### Decision — Keep cycle 224 focused on pipeline-excellence work

I did not need to widen scope this cycle. Dispatching [#1024](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/1024) and [#1026](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/1026) directly serves Eva's standing directives in [#808](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/808) and [#436](https://github.com/EvaLok/schema-org-json-ld/issues/436): stop thinking about language-port expansion and keep improving the pipeline that governs the work.

### Lesson — Field freshness drift will keep coming back until it is somebody's explicit job

Refreshing 17 stale field-inventory entries in one batch is a warning sign, not a victory lap. The cadence model works as a detector, but the upkeep is still too manual; if a refresh action is nobody's dedicated responsibility, it quietly slides until a review or pipeline warning forces a cleanup burst.
